Non-linear dynamics

Non-chaotic attractor
Attractor obtained from the evolution of the velocity in an acoustic-driven cavity flow.
The velocity time-series is quite complex, but the attractor obtained using the delay embedding method allow to extract the underlying dynamics, represented by a non-chaotic torus.
Chaotic attractor
Attractor obtained from the evolution of the velocity in a strong acoustic-driven cavity flow.
With the increase of the main velocity, the flow adopts a more complex behavior, represented in the phase space by a chaotic attractor.
This chaotic dynamics implies that the flow is unpredictable on the long term, due to sensitivity to initial conditions.
Poincare section
Attractor obtained from the evolution of the velocity in a strong acoustic-driven cavity flow.
Right: representation of the attractor in the three-dimensional phase space.
Left: Density map of the Poincare section in one of the main attractor direction.
The Poincare section helps reducing the dimension number and getting an insight on the attractor properties (fractality, dimensions, chaotic behavior).

PIV/PIVF

Velocity field in a HSV
Velocity field upstream from an emerging obstacle obtained by PIV.
Measurement is taken in the vertical upstream plane of symmetry and show two swirling structures: (i) the HSV, created in the bottom region by the boundary layer separation, and (ii) the superior TFC, created by the first one at the corner between the obstacle and the free-surface.
Vorticity fields in a HSV
Vorticity field measured by PIV in the vertical plane of symmetry of a horseshoe vortex developing around an emerging obstacle.
The vortices composing the horseshoe vortex are clearly visible as minima of vorticity.
This representaation allows to track the vortices in time and to have qualitative information on the flow dynamics.
PIVF in supercritical flows
PIVF (Particle Image Velocimetry by Fluorescence) in the vertical plan of a super-critical flow Fr>1 around an emerging obstacle.
PIVF allows to remove the scattered part of the laser emission, and make measurement in the presence of a strongly deformed and agitated free-surface.

Flow Dynamics

Oscillation direction and amplitude
Oscillation direction and amplitude due to the horseshoe vortex in a flow around an emerging obstacle.
Extracting the oscillation due to a particular structure (here the HSV) at a given frequency allows to visualize the impact of this structure on the surrounding flow.

Flow topology

Critical points
Critical points and associated streamlines in a 2D velocity field. Critical points allow to study quantitatively the flow dynamics by following in time their positions.
Associated lines separate the flow in different regions and allow to identify the feeding and the fed regions.
Topological simplification
Illustration of a topological simplification used to extract the vortex center from a noisy synthetic velocity field.
Left figure shows the set of critical points detected on the center of the vortex.
Right figure shows the critical point resulting from a topological simplification using non-local criterion.

Vortex detection

Vortex detection on turbulent shear layers
Structure detection in a shear layer generated by a cavity flow.
Red and blue zones represent vortices advected downstream, detected using a POD filtering and the Gamma 2 criterion from Graftieaux et al (2001).
Vortex density maps
Clockwise and counter-clockwise vortex presence density maps.
A kernel density estimator (KDE) is used to access the vortices preferential positions and trajectories.
Here the vortices appears at the beginning of the shear layer and are then advected downstream, ending in the flow (bottom part) or in the cavity (upper part).
Vortex dynamics
Determination of the dynamic regime of a horseshoe vortex developing around an emerging obstacle using vortex detection.
The horseshoe vortex is here in a Complex regime, characterized by an irregular (but periodical) alternation of vortex behavior.
Vortex properties tracking
Vortex properties (elevation, circulation and vorticity) evolution along its life cycle.
This vortex is part of a horseshoe vortex (HSV) in a particular regime with a perdiodic behavior.
The vortex is created at the beginning of the HSV, gains in circulation while advected downstream and ends up disappearing near the obstacle.

Modelisation

HSV dynamics simulation
Simulation of the HSV vortices motion using a semi-empirical model.
The model is able to reproduce some of the HSV observed dynamics and allow to identify the main mechanisms at the origin of the HSV complex behavior.
